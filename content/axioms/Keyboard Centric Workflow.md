Everything on my computer is based on this concept. I prefer not having to move my hand over to my mouse to do something, I like to be able to control everything — if not most things with just my keyboard. The way this can be achieved is through a bunch of tools. 

For instance, I loved the concept of [[Tiling Window Managers]] which I learned when using [[Arch Linux]]. Sadly Windows doesn't have this out of the box but there are a bunch of third party apps which let us achieve this functionality, such as komorebi and [[GlazeWM]], GlazeWM being the one I use.

I also use [[Neovim]] as my IDE, I tried using [Zed](https://zed.dev/) as it has a nice GUI and supports vim motions, but I just couldn't get used to it, as Neovim felt so much more natural and comfortable for me. Even my [[Quartz]] project promotes this, I have key-binds for every interaction in there and you can freely navigate and explore it without any mouse at all.

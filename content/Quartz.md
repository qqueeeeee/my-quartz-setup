# Quartz

This note is about the site you are looking at right now.

I started with Quartz as a digital garden tool, then kept pushing it until it became something closer to a graph-native portfolio.

## Why this project matters

Most portfolio sites are static and predictable. They show a list of projects, maybe a grid, maybe some screenshots, and that is the whole story.

I wanted something different:

- projects as anchor nodes
- supporting notes as context around those projects
- a graph that feels like exploration instead of decoration

## What I changed

The work here is not just visual. It includes:

- reshaping the graph into the main navigation layer
- building panels so people can browse even without knowing what to search for
- making projects feel more important than ordinary notes
- tuning the site so it stays interactive without becoming too laggy

## What it demonstrates

This project shows a mix of:

- frontend interaction work
- information architecture
- visual direction
- performance tuning
- product thinking about discoverability

I was randomly scrolling YouTube when I came across this video [Interview with Obsidian enthusiast 2026](https://youtu.be/NSTT2iBSwZs?si=PmiKESEHER2a3axc), and I found someone's digital garden. A website built entirely from their Obsidian notes. I remember just staring at the graph view — hundreds of nodes, all with a meaning and a purpose. It was beautiful.

Every single connection in that graph existed because two ideas were actually related. They emerged naturally from the notes themselves, from someone just writing down what they knew and linking it to what they already knew. The graph was a byproduct of genuine thinking.

That is the part I care about most. Not the graph as decoration, but the graph as evidence that the writing underneath it is alive.

Now I am trying to build my own version of that.

This site is where that idea turned into something bigger than a normal notebook:

- part digital garden
- part portfolio
- part map of the things I am actively learning

# How I use Obsidian

The way I use Obsidian is mostly based on [Steph Ango's workflow](https://stephango.com/vault), especially the part where the vault stays simple and the structure comes more from links than folders.

The main parts I took from it are:

- properties/frontmatter
- tags instead of relying too much on folders
- finding notes through quick switching instead of clicking through a file tree

That fits me way better than trying to build some giant perfectly nested folder system.

## My current structure

At this point the vault is set up more like this:

- most notes live in the root
- `References` is for things outside my own world
- `Clippings` is for stuff other people wrote
- `Templates`, `Daily`, and `Attachments` are just support folders
- `Categories` exists as an overview layer, not as the main source of truth

That feels way better to me than having folders try to explain everything.

## Why this works for me

I want writing notes to feel light.

If capturing something feels annoying, I just will not do it consistently. So the system has to be simple enough that I can throw thoughts in fast, but structured enough that I can still find them later.

That is also why I lean so hard into keyboard use here too. I use [[Neovim|vim]] motions, and that connects pretty directly to my [[Keyboard Centric Workflow]].

## Templates

I use a bunch of templates because otherwise I know I will get lazy with organization.

Templates help me:

- add basic metadata without thinking about it
- keep project notes and learning notes somewhat consistent
- make links and tags easier to maintain over time

That matters even more now because this vault is not just private notes anymore, it is also the base for [[Quartz]] and the graph around it.

If I want the longer version of the rules, that is in [[Vault Style Guide]].
